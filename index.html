Access to Care — Appointment Delay & Transportation Barriers
Priority #1 · 34% delayed care · 15% no PCP · disability + foreign-born most affected
High NEMT Relevance►

Key data: 34% of adults delayed care. Top reason: “not being able to get an appointment.” Transportation compounds this for residents with disabilities (9.7%) and foreign-born residents (11.1%). PCP gap improved from 22% in 2018 to 15% in 2024, but 15% of 164,784 = ~24,700 without a primary care provider.

NEMT connection: Each prevented transport-related no-show directly addresses CHNA Priority #1. SDOH Z-code documentation (Z75.3 — Transportation Barrier) creates a simultaneous evidence chain for IRS Schedule H and CRA community development classification.

Mayo advantage: FindHelp is already embedded in Mayo’s EHR (added 2022). NEMT enrollment can be built into the existing FindHelp workflow — reducing care coordinator burden and accelerating the documentation trail examiners need.

Med. NEMT Relevance►

Rochester Epidemiology Project — Mayo’s Unique Attribution Infrastructure
Mayo-only asset · Longitudinal data since 1966 · 56M+ patient records in Mayo Platform
Unique to Mayo►

What it is: The Rochester Epidemiology Project (REP) is a population-based data linkage system covering Olmsted County residents, operated with Mayo Clinic since 1966. The 2025 CHNA explicitly used REP to show health indicator trends — a methodological advantage no other local health system can replicate.

NEMT attribution: REP’s linked records enable NEMT outcomes studies with longitudinal population controls unavailable elsewhere. A propensity-matched cohort study produces publication-quality evidence at a fraction of a de novo RCT cost — transforming the program from a community service into a real-world evidence platform.

Board framing: “This is not just a transportation program — it is a peer-reviewed publication opportunity that distinguishes Mayo’s community benefit strategy nationally and documents ROI with the methodological rigor Mayo Clinic’s research reputation demands.”

Source: Rochester Epidemiology Project (mayoclinic.org) · Mayo Clinic Platform (56M+ records) · 2025 CHNA process description

CDC PHI Best Practices Framework — CHNA & Implementation Strategy
CDC/Public Health Institute (2012) · 8 best practice dimensions · Commissioned by IRS to inform 501(r) regulations
Expand ▾

The CDC/Public Health Institute commissioned this framework (Best Practices for Community Health Needs Assessment and Implementation Strategy Development, Barnett DrPH, Feb 2012) at the IRS's direct request following passage of the ACA. It defines best practice across the full community health improvement cycle and remains the foundational reference for evaluating CHNA process quality.

1. Shared Ownership
Health as a shared community responsibility across sectors — not solely a hospital obligation
2. Community Engagement
Stakeholders as equal partners with shared accountability, not just sources of input
3. Data Collection & Analysis
SDOH data, GIS mapping, sub-county analysis; community members engaged in data collection
4. Priority Setting
Structured, transparent methods (Hanlon, PEARL) with diverse stakeholder participation
5. Alignment
IS strategies aligned with ACA requirements, national health reform, and population health capacity
6. Monitoring & Evaluation
Ongoing tracking with community-defined metrics; longitudinal data to measure collective impact
7. Strategic Investment
Public/private funding alignment; leveraging hospital CB investments with philanthropy and government resources
8. Public Reporting
Transparent, accessible reporting at local/state/national levels; moving beyond minimum compliance
Mayo's 2025 CHNA in CDC PHI Context
The 2025 process demonstrates alignment across all eight dimensions: shared ownership via 30+ coalition partners (dimension 1); multi-method community engagement including co-design and convenience surveys (dimensions 2–3); structured prioritization of 35 indicators to 3 priorities (dimension 4); IS aligned with ACA 501(r) (dimension 5); REP longitudinal data for tracking (dimension 6); CRA bank partnership as strategic investment (dimension 7); publicly accessible ArcGIS StoryMap (dimension 8).
Source: Barnett K. Best Practices for Community Health Needs Assessment and Implementation Strategy Development. CDC/Public Health Institute. February 2012.
